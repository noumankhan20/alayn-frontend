Who sees what

The sidebar renders conditionally from the logged-in role in Redux state. Three tiers of visibility, from full master analytics down to a single personal terminal.

TENANT_OWNER · SUPER_ADMIN
Owner & Admin

Full master view across every branch. Redirected straight to the dashboard on login.

  • Overview — master P&L & forecasting
  • Location Manager — branches & outlets
  • Workforce & Scheduler — staff, shifts, leave
  • Smart Inventory & Procurement — stock & POs
  • Menu Manager — categories & pricing
  • POS & Kitchen Operations
  • Support Tickets & Feedback
  • Waste Management
  • Settings — billing & business info
MANAGER
Outlet Manager

Everything scoped to their assigned outlet — no cross-branch or master-financial access.

  • Overview — their outlet only
  • Workforce Directory — shifts & staff
  • Inventory Adjustments — counts & low-stock
  • POS & Kitchen Operations
  • Support Tickets — outlet queries
  • Waste Logs — create & review
STAFF · KITCHEN
Staff & Kitchen

A focused, personal view — the terminal and calendar each role needs, nothing more.

  • Shift Calendar — personal shifts & swaps
  • Leave Request — submit & track
  • POS Terminal — staff only
  • Kitchen Dispatch Board — kitchen only
  • Attendance Logs — punch in / out
01
Foundation · Auth & Scoping

Authentication & Core Setup

Manages user identity, session persistence, and the routing and role logic that every other module depends on.

feeds RBAC for every sidebar
feeds Router guards on all dashboard routes
provides active user & role in Redux
Pages
  • /loginSign-in with email & password, error/success banners, and post-auth redirect.
  • /signupMulti-step registration — Step 1 admin account, Step 2 tenant & business details (name, location count, type, contact).
State & API
  • authSlice.tsHolds user + isAuthenticated; restores session on refresh via SSR-safe localStorage check.
  • authApiSlice.tsRTK Query — login, register, logout mutations (token in HTTP-only cookie).
Core behaviour
  • JWT expiry re-auth queueing in baseApi.ts to avoid race conditions on concurrent 401s.
  • Router guards via a withAuth HOC / middleware protecting dashboard routes.
  • Sidebar options render conditionally from the user's role.
Backend connections · auth.routes.ts
POST/api/v1/auth/loginAuth verification
POST/api/v1/auth/registerTenant & admin setup
POST/api/v1/auth/logoutClears cookies
POST/api/v1/auth/refreshAccess-token rotation

How the pieces connect

Foundation (Auth + Outlet) scopes and secures everything. Operations runs the day — staff clock in, stock moves, orders fire to the kitchen, waste is logged back against inventory. Intelligence reads all of that into the owner's dashboard, and Support closes the loop with guests and staff. Inventory sits at the centre: Menu/POS consumes it, Waste corrects it, and the Dashboard forecasts on it.
